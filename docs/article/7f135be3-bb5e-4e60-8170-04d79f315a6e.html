Parliament passes law to allow multiple citizenship for Ukrainians

June 18, 2025
  • Law permits dual or multiple citizenships.
  • Aims to counter war-related population decline.
  • Excludes Russian citizenship for security reasons.
  • Encourages diaspora investment and remittances.
  • Effective bingo Effective early 2026.
Ukraine’s parliament passed a law on June 18, 2025, permitting multiple citizenship to address a demographic crisis triggered by Russia’s ongoing war and to strengthen ties with its diaspora. The legislation aims to counter population decline, with millions displaced since the conflict’s escalation in 2022. By allowing dual or multiple citizenships, Ukraine seeks to retain connections with citizens abroad, particularly in Europe and North America, where many have resettled. The law also responds to economic pressures, encouraging diaspora investment and remittances, which the World Bank estimates contributed $15 billion to Ukraine’s economy in 2024. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy supported the measure, framing it as a step toward national resilience. However, the law excludes Russian citizenship to maintain security amid the war. Critics argue it could complicate legal frameworks, but supporters see it as vital for demographic and economic stability. The move aligns with Ukraine’s broader strategy to integrate with Western institutions, including EU membership aspirations. Posts on X indicate mixed public sentiment, with some praising the reform and others questioning its implementation. The law is expected to take effect in early 2026, pending final approvals. Additional research highlights similar policies in countries like Poland, which have bolstered diaspora engagement. The legislation marks a significant shift in Ukraine’s citizenship policy, reflecting the war’s profound societal impact.
