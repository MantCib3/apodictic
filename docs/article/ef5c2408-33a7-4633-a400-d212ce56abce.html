Man shot by Kenyan police during protests is in intensive care, father says

June 18, 2025
  • Man shot at point-blank range by police.
  • Protests target extrajudicial killings in Nairobi.
  • Victim in critical condition, identity undisclosed.
  • Human rights groups condemn excessive police force.
  • Investigations underway amid public outrage.
In Nairobi, a young man shot at point-blank range by a Kenyan police officer during protests against extrajudicial killings is in intensive care, his father reported. The incident occurred amid heightened tensions in the capital, where demonstrators gathered to protest police violence. The shooting, captured by an Associated Press photographer, has fueled public outrage and renewed calls for accountability within Kenya’s security forces. Protests erupted following a series of alleged extrajudicial killings, with citizens demanding reforms to address systemic issues in law enforcement. The victim, whose identity remains undisclosed, was critically injured, underscoring the volatile nature of the demonstrations. Authorities have faced scrutiny for their handling of protests, with human rights groups condemning excessive force. The Kenyan police have yet to issue a formal statement regarding the incident, but investigations are reportedly underway. This event follows a pattern of unrest, as seen in earlier 2025 protests where security forces were implicated in violent crackdowns. The broader context includes public frustration over governance and economic challenges, amplifying the protests’ intensity. International observers, including the UN, have urged restraint and dialogue to prevent further escalation. The incident has also sparked discussions on social media platforms like X, with users expressing solidarity with the victim and criticizing police tactics. As the situation develops, the focus remains on the victim’s recovery and the government’s response to demands for justice.
