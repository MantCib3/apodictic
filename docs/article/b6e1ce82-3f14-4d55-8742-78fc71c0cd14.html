Honda-backed Helm.ai unveils vision system for self-driving cars

June 19, 2025
  • Helm.ai Vision enhances self-driving in urban settings.
  • System integrates with Nvidia and Qualcomm platforms.
  • Honda’s 0 series EVs will feature the technology.
  • Helm.ai raised $102 million from investors.
  • Technology aims to improve safety and efficiency.
On June 19, 2025, Honda-backed Helm.ai introduced Helm.ai Vision, a camera-based system designed to enhance self-driving capabilities in urban environments. The technology, which integrates with platforms like Nvidia and Qualcomm, enables automakers to incorporate advanced vision systems into mass-market vehicles. Helm.ai has raised $102 million from investors, including Goodyear Ventures, and is in talks with multiple automakers for broader adoption. The system creates a bird’s-eye view using multiple cameras, improving vehicle planning and control. It will be integrated into Honda’s 0 series electric vehicles, starting with the 0 SUV, set for U.S. production. CEO Vlad Voroninski emphasized the system’s vision-based approach, distinguishing it from radar and LiDAR-heavy systems. The technology aims to revolutionize city driving by enhancing safety and efficiency. Despite challenges like regulatory hurdles and high costs, Helm.ai’s partnerships signal strong industry confidence. The system’s compatibility with existing vehicle technologies offers a promising path for autonomous driving advancements.
Helm.ai's technology is primarily based on vision.
Vlad Voroninski
