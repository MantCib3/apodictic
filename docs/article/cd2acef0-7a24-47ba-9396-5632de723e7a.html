Airbus upbeat, but may have to wait for AirAsia deal

June 18, 2025
  • AirAsia delays expected Airbus deal.
  • U.S. tariffs increase aircraft component costs.
  • Airbus secures 50 A320neo orders.
  • AirAsia may order A321neo by late 2025.
  • Air show highlights fuel-efficient aircraft.
At the world’s largest air show on June 18, 2025, Airbus expressed optimism about future deals despite tempered expectations for an immediate agreement with AirAsia, a key budget airline client. AirAsia’s owner, Capital A, known for dramatic last-minute deals, downplayed prospects of imminent contracts with Airbus or rival Embraer. Airbus, however, remains confident in its long-term partnership with AirAsia, citing strong demand for its A320neo and A330neo aircraft in the Asia-Pacific region. The air show, a major industry event, showcased Airbus’s latest innovations, including fuel-efficient models to counter rising operational costs amid global tariff pressures. AirAsia’s hesitation stems from economic uncertainties, including U.S. tariffs impacting aircraft component costs, which have risen by 10-15% since January 2025, according to industry reports. These tariffs, intensified under President Trump, have strained airline budgets, forcing carriers to delay fleet expansions. Despite this, Airbus secured orders for 50 A320neo aircraft from other regional carriers, signaling robust market interest. Analysts on X note AirAsia’s cautious approach reflects broader industry concerns about inflation and supply chain disruptions. Airbus is also exploring sustainable aviation fuel solutions to mitigate long-term cost pressures. Additional research indicates AirAsia may finalize a smaller order by late 2025, focusing on cost-effective A321neo models. The air show concluded with Airbus emphasizing innovation and resilience in a volatile market.
